Daomiao (Daniel) Wang

Ph.D. Candidate  ·  Dept. of Biomedical Engineering, Fudan University

I am a Ph.D. Candidate (expected 2027) in the Department of Biomedical Engineering (AI for Healthcare) at Fudan University, advised by Prof. Cuiwei Yang and Prof. Xin Li at Duke University. My research sits at the intersection of Trustworthy AI for Healthcare, Human-Robot/AI Collaboration, and Embodied AI, with a focus on building translatable AI systems for physiological signal understanding and robot-assisted rehabilitation in real-world clinical workflows.

My work spans the full stack from data construction and model design to clinical validation, with hands-on experience across deep learning architectures from CNNs and Transformers to large language models. I am particularly interested in agentic AI systems, scenario-driven computing, and human–AI collaboration.

Selected Projects

Key Contributor · NSFC + National Key R&D
Trustworthy AI Framework for AF Cardiac Function Management
2023.09 – 2027.06 (Doctoral Thesis)
End-to-end trustworthy healthcare AI framework addressing signal quality, privacy, interpretability, and clinical semantic interaction for continuous AF assessment. Encompasses SDPR, PM²ECGCN, IHemoMIL, and PISMIL sub-systems.
Outcome: 3 first-author papers (PR, ESWA, BioCAS); 2 under review; 1 patent.
Principal Investigator · Industry Collaboration
Gaze-Driven Closed-Loop Robot-Assisted Neurorehabilitation
2024.09 – 2025.09
Closed-loop paradigm using visual attention for gaze-based intent decoding and upper-limb stroke rehabilitation. Full eye-tracking system developed; sensorless adaptive control algorithm validated via fNIRS brain imaging.
Outcome: 1 ICRA paper (top-tier robotic conference); Class II medical device registration; commercial sales.
Principal Investigator · Academia–Industry–Clinical
Scenario-Driven Embodied Agent for Rehabilitation Diagnosis & Treatment
2025.09 – 2026.06
Edge-computing Foundation Model with QLoRA + RAG for scenario-driven reasoning. Full-chain "perception–decision–execution" via ReAct-Reflexion on LangGraph, integrating Tool Use and safety guardrails. Collaboration with Duke University and Fuwai Hospital.
Outcome: 1 AEI paper (submitted); prototype under clinical validation.
