Ph.D. Candidate  ·  Dept. of Biomedical Engineering, Fudan University

I am a Ph.D. Candidate (expected 2027) in the Department of Biomedical Engineering at Fudan University. My research lies at the intersection of AI for Healthcare, Ubiquitous Computing, and Embodied AI, with a focus on building trustworthy, clinically translatable AI systems for physiological signal analysis and robot-assisted rehabilitation.

My work spans the full pipeline from data construction and model design to clinical validation and commercial deployment. I have hands-on experience across the CNN→Transformer→LLM evolution, and am particularly interested in agentic AI systems, interpretable machine learning, and human–robot interaction in medical contexts.

Selected Projects

Project Image
Key Contributor · NSFC + National Key R&D
Trustworthy AI Framework for AF Cardiac Function Management
2023.09 – 2027.06 (Doctoral Thesis)
End-to-end trustworthy healthcare AI framework addressing signal quality, privacy, interpretability, and clinical semantic interaction for continuous AF assessment. Encompasses SDPR, PM²ECGCN, IHemoMIL, and PISMIL sub-systems.
Outcome: 3 first-author papers (PR, ESWA, BioCAS); 2 under review; 1 patent.
Project Image
Principal Investigator · Industry Collaboration
Gaze-Driven Closed-Loop Robot-Assisted Neurorehabilitation
2024.09 – 2025.09
Closed-loop paradigm using visual attention for gaze-based intent decoding and upper-limb stroke rehabilitation. Full eye-tracking system developed; sensorless adaptive control algorithm validated via fNIRS brain imaging.
Outcome: 1 ICRA paper; Class II medical device registration; commercial sales.
Project Image
Principal Investigator · Academia–Industry–Clinical
Scenario-Driven Embodied Agent for Rehabilitation Diagnosis & Treatment
2025.09 – 2026.06
Edge-computing Foundation Model with QLoRA + RAG for scenario-driven reasoning. Full-chain "perception–decision–execution" via ReAct-Reflexion on LangGraph, integrating Tool Use and safety guardrails. Collaboration with Duke University, Fuwai Hospital, and ZDriod Medical.
Outcome: 1 AEI paper (submitted); prototype under clinical validation.
